Explanation

On this page you will find the main results of the models from the project. The two models fitted were a topic model and a polarisation model.

The topic model chart has the date on the x-axis, and the frequency of topic on the y-axis. Displayed on the chart are the top 10 most frequent topics over the period from 1945 until present. To display only one topic, double-click on the corresponding legend key on the right of the chart. To go back to displaying all topics, double-click on any of the keys.

The polarisation model chart has the date on the x-axis, and the polarisation score on the y-axis. Displayed on the chart are the 3 major parties of the UK (Liberal Democrats, Labour and Conservatives). As with the topic chart, double-click on the party you want to display in isolation.

Topic Chart Explanation

The first chart on the page displays the top ten most frequent topics over time according to the topic model. For example, we can see on the chart that Northern Ireland is the most frequently discussed topic, with a peak in frequency in 1998 around the Good Friday Agreement.

Per Decade Topic Chart Explanation

There is a simple form to select the decade to view. This enables you to view the most popular topics per decade. For example, in the 1970s unions were a frequently discussed topic. In fact, it was more frequently discussed in the 1970s than in any other decade.

Polarisation Chart Explanation

The final plot is of the polarisation model results over time. All three major political parties are displayed on the same chart. On the x-axis is 'year' and on the y-axis the left-right score. We observe a peak in LibDem rightness in the early 1970s.
